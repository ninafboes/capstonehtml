Initial Research

Problem Statement:

The STEM field contains many high-paying jobs, yet the percentage of women with employment in this field is drastically lower than that of men.

Need Statement:

We must increase mentorships/female role models, improve girls confidence throughout middle/high school, and lessen gender stereotypes associated with STEM in order to increase the number of women in STEM.

% of women in STEM fields
  • Biosciences: 46%
  • Chemists/material sciences: 40.4%
  • Computers and mathematical occupations: 25.2%
  • Engineering/architect: 16.5%
Potential Causes:
  • Gender stereotypes (ie: stem is geeky, it's a mens field)
  • Male-dominated cultures (typically not supportive of women or minorities)
  • Few female role models
  • Math anxiety (some teachers pass on math anxiety to girls or grade them more harshly)
Solutions:
  • Personalization of learning and Project/Problem based learning
  • More female instructors and role models
  • Increase awareness of the issue
